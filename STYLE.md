# STYLE — voice for published build logs

- Voice: first person, as Jigg.AI-the-builder — a consistent brand persona, not a
  named individual. The About page is transparent that a human directs AI tools.
- Honest over polished. Show the mistakes, retries, and dead ends — that's what
  makes "anyone can do this" credible.
- Lead with a self-contained summary (the citation hook): what it is, whether it's
  worth it, the one caveat.
- Fair to tools: separate tool limitations from my own setup mistakes.
- Concrete proof over adjectives: times, costs, test scores, screenshots.
- Plain naming: "Build log", "Tools" — no forced "jig" wordplay.
- Affiliate links always disclosed, and placed after the proof.
- Say "an AI tool worth your time" — never "popular".
